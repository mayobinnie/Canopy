Common Questions

What operators need to know

Which sites in the Humber corridor does Canopy serve?+
Canopy serves all major CNI and industrial sites in the Humber Estuary corridor. This includes VPI Immingham CHP, the Humber Refinery Complex, Port of Immingham (ABP), Port of Hull (ABP), Siemens Gamesa's blade manufacturing facility at Alexandra Dock, the Grimsby Offshore Wind Hub, the Viking CCS pipeline corridor, HMP Humber, the National Grid substation network in Yorkshire and Lincolnshire, and the offshore wind O&M bases at Grimsby and Hull. We also serve commercial and industrial estates across East Yorkshire, the East Riding and North Lincolnshire.
What is the mobilisation time for Humber sites?+
For standard planned survey and inspection engagements, Canopy mobilises to Humber sites in three to five working days — allowing time for RAMS documentation, insurance certificates, ATC notifications and site access compliance. For urgent requirements including post-incident assessment, insurance survey or time-critical inspection, we can mobilise to Humber sites within 24 hours subject to availability and weather conditions.
Does Canopy have experience with the Humber's active aerodrome environment?+
Yes. The Humber Estuary includes complex airspace with Humberside Airport, Lincolnshire airspace and the active aerodrome environments at several industrial sites. Canopy has specific experience managing ATC coordination for drone operations in the Humber corridor, including NOTAM filing, drone assist consultation and pre-flight ATC notification protocols.
Can Canopy serve sites on both sides of the Humber?+
Yes. Canopy serves sites on both the North Lincolnshire side of the estuary — including Immingham, Grimsby and Scunthorpe — and the East Yorkshire and Hull side. For sites requiring operations on both sides of the estuary, we plan each zone as a separate flight area.
Does Canopy offer annual survey programmes for Humber operators?+
Yes. Annual thermal survey, LiDAR and photogrammetry programmes are available for Humber operators with multi-site estates. Repeat surveys at 12-month intervals enable change detection against the baseline dataset and provide the documented evidence trail required for SECR reporting, insurance purposes and NIS2 compliance. Preferential rates are available for annual programme clients.
