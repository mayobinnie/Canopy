How It Works

The Canopy process

Every engagement follows a structured methodology that ensures accurate data, legally defensible outputs and actionable results.

01
Site Capture

Drone photogrammetry and LiDAR survey of the site interior and exterior. RTK GNSS georeferencing throughout. Capture plan designed to achieve required model resolution and coverage.

02
Point Cloud Processing

Dense photogrammetric point cloud and LiDAR returns merged, cleaned and georeferenced. All data processed to survey-grade accuracy with full quality documentation.

03
Model Construction

Textured 3D mesh built from combined datasets. Interior and exterior elements modelled. Access routes, plant locations, hazard zones and infrastructure incorporated.

04
Delivery and Integration

Digital twin delivered in open formats compatible with your existing infrastructure. GeoTIFF, LAS, OBJ and IFC-compatible files. No proprietary platform required.

Common Questions

What operators need to know

What is a digital twin?+
A digital twin is a centimetre-accurate 3D virtual replica of a physical asset or site, built from real survey data rather than design drawings. It captures the as-built condition of every structural element, space and external feature, and is georeferenced to enable spatial analysis, measurement and integration with GIS and BIM systems. Unlike a rendered visualisation, a digital twin is a survey-grade dataset with documented positional accuracy.
How is a digital twin different from a BIM model?+
A BIM model is built from design drawings and represents design intent. A digital twin is generated from real drone capture and represents the actual as-built condition of the asset. For existing infrastructure and buildings that predate BIM, a digital twin is often the only way to create an accurate 3D asset record. Digital twins also capture condition data that BIM cannot — including thermal anomalies, surface deterioration and structural deformation.
What formats is the digital twin delivered in?+
Canopy digital twins are delivered in open, industry-standard formats. 3D mesh in OBJ format for visualisation and BIM integration. Dense point cloud in LAS format for GIS and specialist analysis platforms. Georeferenced orthoimage in GeoTIFF format. IFC-compatible export for BIM workflows. All data is delivered as standard files — no proprietary platform is required to access any deliverable.
How long does digital twin generation take?+
Timeline depends on site size and complexity. A standard industrial building requires one to two days of capture and two to three weeks of processing. A large multi-building estate requires a phased capture programme with processing delivered incrementally. Full timelines are provided at scoping stage.
Can you model building interiors as well as exteriors?+
Yes. Canopy captures both interior and exterior environments where access is available. Interior capture uses a combination of terrestrial photogrammetry and ground-level LiDAR to model rooms, corridors, stairwells and plant areas. Combined with exterior drone capture, the result is a seamless interior-exterior model of the complete asset.
