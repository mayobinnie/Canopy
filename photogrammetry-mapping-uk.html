How It Works

The Canopy process

Every engagement follows a structured methodology that ensures accurate data, legally defensible outputs and actionable results.

01
GCP Setup

Ground Control Points established using Emlid RS3 RTK GNSS for centimetre-level positional accuracy. GCP placement designed to optimise accuracy across the full survey area.

02
Aerial Capture

Systematic overlapping imagery captured at optimised altitude for the required ground sampling distance. Full coverage with appropriate sidelap ensuring no data gaps.

03
Photogrammetric Processing

Agisoft Metashape Professional processes all imagery into dense point cloud, 3D mesh and georeferenced orthomosaic. All outputs aligned to the GCP coordinate system.

04
Data Delivery

All processed datasets delivered in open, industry-standard formats. Full technical report including accuracy assessment, GCP coordinates and processing parameters.

Common Questions

What operators need to know

What is photogrammetry survey?+
Photogrammetry is the science of making measurements from photographs. In drone survey, overlapping aerial images are captured systematically over the survey area and processed using photogrammetric software to produce georeferenced 2D and 3D outputs — including orthomosaic imagery, dense point clouds and 3D mesh models. The process uses the overlap between images and known ground control positions to calculate accurate 3D geometry across the survey area.
What accuracy can I expect from a drone photogrammetry survey?+
With RTK GNSS ground control, Canopy typically achieves horizontal accuracy of 1-2cm and vertical accuracy of 2-3cm across the survey area. Actual accuracy depends on flying altitude, ground control point density and distribution, and terrain characteristics. An accuracy report is provided with every deliverable, documenting GCP residuals and reprojection error.
What outputs are delivered from a photogrammetry survey?+
Standard Canopy photogrammetry deliverables include: a georeferenced orthomosaic (GeoTIFF) suitable for GIS and planning use; a dense 3D point cloud (LAS format) for structural analysis and volumetric measurement; a textured 3D mesh (OBJ format) for visualisation and digital twin use; and a technical report including accuracy assessment and processing parameters. Additional outputs including DSM, DTM and contour data are available on request.
How long does a photogrammetry survey take?+
Survey duration depends on area size and required ground sampling distance. A typical 50-hectare industrial estate requires half a day to one day of capture operations. Processing typically takes three to five working days. Larger areas are delivered on a programme basis with interim milestones. All timelines include weather contingency.
Can photogrammetry data be combined with thermal survey data?+
Yes — and this is a significant advantage of using Canopy for both services. Photogrammetric and thermal datasets share the same RTK georeferencing, enabling direct spatial alignment of the two datasets. Thermal anomaly locations can be overlaid on the 3D model and orthomosaic, giving facilities managers precise location data for every finding in the context of the building geometry.
